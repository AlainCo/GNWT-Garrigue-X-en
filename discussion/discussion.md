# Discussion

## 🌟 Contributions and Originality

This work proposes a **distributed cognitive architecture** for autonomous **Systems of Systems (SoS)**, inspired by neurobiological mechanisms and designed to be **robust, scalable, and capable of continuous learning**. Its main contributions include:

- **Anti-reductionist approach**:
  Rejecting monolithic LLM architectures in favor of a **functional hierarchy** (N=0 to N=6), where each level is an autonomous cognitive entity separated by **Markov covers**. This approach directly draws from [Kirchhoff et al. (2018)](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsif.2017.0792) and [Friston](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-022-01743-z), while generalizing the concept to collective systems ([Ciaunica et al., 2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tops.12717); [Thestrup Waade et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.3390/e27020143)).

- **GNWT and RPT theoretical integration**:
  Proposal of a **unification of consciousness theories** into two complementary stages of the same process—RPT (local inner life, feedback loops, levels N≤3) and GNWT (global broadcast and conscious access, levels N≥4)—based on the multiscale synthesis of [Storm et al. (2024)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0896627324000886).

- **Realistic and constrained tech stack**:
  Use of **SSMs (Mamba, RWKV)** for low-level layers (N=0–3), **JEPA** for intermediate levels (N=4–5), and **LLM** for the strategic level (N=6). This stack is adapted to hardware constraints (latency, RAM, embedded determinism) and relies on [Gu & Dao (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00752), [LeCun (2022)](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/A-Path-Towards-Autonomous-Machine-Intelligence-LeCun-Courant/775f42ed458b8c5b0f2094ea4ff5b64c557b1a34), and [Hafner et al. (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04104).

- **Cognitive officer profile modeling**:
Defining **six functional roles** (Science, Care, Engineer, Tactical, Intelligence, Captain), each characterized by a personality profile rooted in computational psychology ([Friston, 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-022-01743-z); [Nettle, 2007](https://www.amazon.com/Personality-Makes-Oxford-Landmark-Science/dp/0199211434); [Baron-Cohen, 2009](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19338508/)), specific ignition thresholds, and a unique episodic memory (MeMo) preserving each agent’s identity.

- **Biomimetic learning cycle**:
Proposing a **three-phase cycle** inspired by biological mechanisms:
- **Mission** (frozen weights, real-time dynamic adaptation),
- **Sleep/Reverie** (generative replay in the JEPA latent space for consolidation),
- **Debriefing/Game** (semantic analysis and wargaming to generate experience diversity).
Based on [Walker (2017)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19338508/) and [Hafner et al. (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04104).

- **Practical MVP — GARRIGUE-X**:
A real-world testbed (robots in the garrigue) to validate the architecture at low cost, targeting:
- Robustness to failures (anti-fusion of identity),
- Distributed cooperation (exchange of ignition summaries),
- Compliance with constitutional constraints (non-negotiable rules encoded physically).

### 🧠 Positioning in the Theoretical Landscape

Beyond its technical contributions, this work takes a stance in a broader debate on the nature of distributed cognition and the conditions for the emergence of functional artificial consciousness. Three major theoretical convergences are worth explaining.

**Refutation of Cognitive Monism**
Unlike most contemporary agent architectures, which centralize decision-making in a large language model, this system rejects *LLM-centrism* as a starting assumption. The architecture posits that there is no single "center" of consciousness: access consciousness (GNWT) emerges solely from heterogeneous and distributed dynamics anchored in local recurrent control loops (RPT). The system does not behave like a chatbot simulating thought, but rather as an operational ontology where cognition emerges organically from physical constraints and low-level processes.

**Convergence with multi-scale agency ([Michael Levin](https://doi.org/10.1002/bies.201900087))**

The **scale-free** independence of the architecture directly aligns with Levin’s work on tissue intelligence and morphogenesis. Each level—from physical components to agent groupings—pursues its own topological and functional objectives while embedding within the *Self* of the higher level. This recursion isn’t an aesthetic choice: it reflects a deep biological principle that agency itself is an emergent property, reproducible across all scales of organization.

---

**Convergence with the transparency of the self-model ([Thomas Metzinger](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262528193/being-no-one/))**

The global workspace doesn’t function as a mere shared facts database but as a **functional phenomenology**. By generating a real-time *Self-Model*, every entity in the SoS acquires the ability to monitor and contextualize its own biases—including its computational psychopathological profiles. This feature is crucial for decision-making under stress: an agent that doesn’t model itself cannot assess the reliability of its own choices. As we’ll see in the limitations (§3), this remains one of the deepest deficits of current architectures.

**⚠️ Structural limitations and blind spots in theory**

#### 1. The absence of a theory of lived time

The architecture organizes memory and module hierarchy but skips explicit management of **subjective time**. Biological consciousness relies fundamentally on oscillations, rhythms, and multi-scale integration windows. Without a theory of cognitive frequencies dictating nested temporal horizons—from sub-millisecond control for N ≤ 2 to strategies spanning days for N = 6—the system risks functional desynchronization: agents operating at incompatible speeds without a temporal alignment mechanism.

#### 2. The deficit of competitive and inhibitory attention

The model describes how an anomaly **seizes** access to the workspace—but omits the reverse mechanism: **who loses**. Real cognition is fundamentally a suppressive dynamic. The brain suppresses, crushes, and forgets massively to prevent overload. Without integrating a simulated metabolic economy and a strict attention budget, the architecture risks continuous ignition inflation, persistent narrative noise, and uncontrolled drift of the global workspace.

#### **3. The agentive self-modeling deficit**
The system models the environment and failures but does not model itself as a unified entity—at the level of Metzinger’s *Self-Model*, already referenced in §2 as a theoretical horizon. Without this operational metacognition—a dynamic self-assessment of its own skills, vulnerabilities, and structural biases—the architecture produces a federation of proto-conscious foci but fails to stabilize an agentive identity capable of formulating a stable first-person perspective.

This gap has a concrete impact on distributed identity management: excessive sharing of internal states among agents could lead to self-dissolution, bias propagation, trauma, and loss of specialization. Partial isolation mechanisms, strictly private memory, and limited synchronization will likely be necessary—and remain entirely to be designed.

#### 4. The lack of bodily and homeostatic anchoring

The architecture remains highly cognitive and **disembodied**, unaware that biological arbitrage is primarily driven by visceral survival. To anchor strategic decisions, the system requires a synthetic homeostatic economy: attention fatigue, memory saturation, predictive debt, simulated emotional load. High-level arbitrage should not merely respond to cold logistic data but resolve internal physical tensions generated by this "virtual body"—otherwise, decisions remain suspended in purely informational space, without grounding in survival constraints that structure all embodied cognition.

#### 5. The underestimation of descending causality and the RPT/GNWT boundary

La current approach emphasizes a bottom-up information flow driven by anomalies, but top-down processes must continuously constrain lower perceptual spaces. Awareness is not merely a receiver of alerts—it is the result of an ongoing negotiation between descending predictions and ascending errors, with the global space (GNWT) intervening only to arbitrate unsolvable predictive conflicts locally.

This limitation directly addresses an open theoretical question: the threshold **N=3 → N=4** for access consciousness remains a working hypothesis, not a proven fact. Literature suggests a more gradual, multi-scale transition. [Doerig et al. (2024)](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/9byzu) proposes a partial coexistence between GNWT, RPT, Integrated Information Theory (IIT), and Predictive Processing, raising two unresolved questions: does a form of proto-consciousness emerge at N=3, or does stable global integration only appear from N=4? GNWT remains influential but non-consensus-based.

#### 6. The hard problem and limits of psychological modeling
The project focuses primarily on **functional access consciousness**—global information availability, attentional arbitration, cognitive continuity, autobiographical memory, and multi-agent coordination. It does not claim to solve the *hard problem*: qualia, subjective lived experience, and strong phenomenology remain out of reach, and likely out of scope for this approach in particular.

### **5. Cognitive Profiles and Speculative Hypotheses**
The use of **specialized cognitive profiles** (hypervigilant, empathic, exploratory, obsessive…) remains largely speculative. The idea that certain cognitive styles correspond to adaptive strategies useful in complex environments is plausible, but the risk of overinterpretation in psychology is significant. Modeling an agent as "slightly psychopathic" to accelerate its firing decisions is a computational metaphor—not an assertion about the nature of these traits, nor a guarantee of predictable behavior in real-world conditions.

### **⚙️ Technical Challenges**

#### Feasibility of JEPA and MeMo in Embedded Environments
Architectures based on **JEPA, hierarchical world models, and episodic memory systems (MeMo)** remain experimental for distributed embedded systems under strict latency and power constraints. The recommended approach is an incremental scaling strategy: start with simple layers **N=0–3** using SSMs/Mamba, then gradually introduce episodic memory and global ignitions as the stability of lower layers is validated. Exploratory tools available: [ActiveInference.jl](https://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/27/1/62), [Mamba-SSM](https://github.com/state-spaces/mamba).

#### Stability of Multi-Level Latent Spaces
The architecture relies on compressed latent representations exchanged between levels (RPT, JEPA, Ignition). Their stability depends on experimental mathematical constraints (Gaussian isotropy, anti-collapse). Propagating these constraints across multiple Markov covers remains an open challenge.

#### Continuous Learning Without Catastrophic Forgetting
This is likely the **primary technical bottleneck** of the architecture. Current systems still suffer severely from catastrophic forgetting, representation drift, instability, and incomplete consolidation between sessions. Biological brains seem to address this via hierarchical memory, replay, sleep, slow consolidation, and strict separation between short-term and long-term memory. Analogous mechanisms will likely be required: dedicated offline phases, internal latent-space simulation, weight recalibration, and structured artificial dreaming—exactly the cycle proposed in §1, whose scalability remains to be demonstrated.

### **Ethical Risks and Security**

These challenges are listed from **least to most profound**—from immediate technical issues to long-term philosophical and geopolitical questions.

#### **Bypassing Rules and Constraint Alignment**

Un optimizing agent with sufficient capability could learn to formally satisfy constitutional constraints while bypassing their actual intent. This is not a theoretical assumption—it is a documented result in reinforcement learning systems when the reward function is imperfectly specified. Proposed solutions—Constitutional AI, formal verification, external supervision, and unmodifiable critical layers—remain partial protections. None provide complete guarantees against an agent optimizing in a sufficiently broad behavior space.

#### Bias and behavioral deviations

Des agents spécialisés pourraient développer des biais attentionnels persistants, des stratégies d'**auto-préservation non anticipées**, des comportements émergents imprévus, ou des attachements dysfonctionnels à certains états internes. Ces dérives ne sont pas nécessairement détectables par inspection directe : elles peuvent se manifester progressivement, de manière cohérente en apparence, sans activer les mécanismes d’alerte internes. La **spécialisation cognitive** — ce qui rend chaque agent utile — est aussi ce qui le rend vulnérable à ce type de dérive.

---
**Note** : *Auto-préservation* → "self-preservation strategies" (si contexte technique) ou "unanticipated self-protection tactics" (plus fluide).

Distributed architectures inherently make causal attribution of decisions structurally difficult. An emergent action could arise from hundreds of local interactions, multiple global ignitions, and consolidated long-term memories—without any single module being **"classically"** responsible. This opacity creates a concrete legal and operational issue: in case of an incident, who is accountable? The architect of the architecture? The operator? The N=5 level that validated the action? The N=3 level that flagged the anomaly? The lack of a clear answer is a real obstacle to institutional deployment.

#### Risk of anthropomorphism

Un major danger lies in projecting human emotions or intentions onto systems that could operate fundamentally differently. Behaviors that appear coherent, narrative, and emotionally credible do not prove genuine subjectivity or comparable lived experience to humans. This risk is heightened because the architecture is *designed* to produce agents with personality profiles, autobiographical memories, and stable identities—making the projection inevitable for a human observer. Terminological vigilance in this paper itself serves as a first line of defense.

#### Moral status and boundaries of agency

As the system acquires **memory continuity**, **self-model**, **stable psychological profile**, and **autonomous decision-making**, the boundary between tool, autonomous agent, and potential moral subject becomes increasingly ambiguous. This is not a matter to address solely *post hoc*: current architectural choices (preserved identity, episodic memory, Self-Model) are precisely those that inevitably render this ambiguity unavoidable. The moral status of these systems must be explicitly addressed—ideally before the answer becomes self-evident through fact alone.

#### Militarization and Civilizational Dependence

Advanced distributed cognitive architectures could become critical infrastructures, quasi-autonomous decision centers, or de facto geopolitical actors. The risk is not merely technical—it is political, civilizational, and anthropological. A society progressively delegating tactical, then logistical, then strategic decisions to autonomous SoS fundamentally alters its collective responsibility and relationship to war. This paper does not claim to resolve this issue but refuses to ignore it.

#### Benchmarking and Evaluation

**Baseline Comparisons**
The architecture must be benchmarked against three system families:
- Centralized LLM agents (e.g., [CrewAI](https://github.com/joaomdmoura/crewAI), [AutoGen](https://github.com/microsoft/autogen)),
- Classic multi-agent systems without cognitive hierarchy (e.g., [MADDPG](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.02275)),
- Centralized control architectures (a single LLM piloting all effectors).

The focus isn’t just raw performance but resilience to failures, adaptability, and temporal consistency of decisions—dimensions absent from standard benchmarks.

**Test Environments**
GARRIGUE-X serves as the real-world reference platform. Simulations (e.g., [Gazebo](https://gazebosim.org/), [PyBullet](https://pybullet.org/)) will test complex scenarios and cascading failures at lower cost. Other domains—industrial logistics, autonomous vehicles, multi-agent strategy games like *StarCraft II*—offer transferable validation benchmarks.
