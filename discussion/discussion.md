# Discussion

## ⭐ Contributions and Originality

This work proposes a **distributed cognitive architecture** for autonomous Systems of Systems (SoS), inspired by neurobiological mechanisms and designed to be **robust, scalable, and capable of continuous learning**. Its main contributions are as follows:

- **Anti-reductionist approach**:
Rejection of monolithic LLM architectures in favor of a **functional hierarchy** (N=0 to N=6), where each level is an autonomous cognitive entity separated by **Markov blankets**. This approach directly draws from the work of [Kirchhoff et al. (2018)](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsif.2017.0792) and [Friston](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-022-01743-z), while generalizing the concept to collective systems ([Ciaunica et al., 2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tops.12717); [Thestrup Waade et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.3390/e27020143)).

- **Theoretical Integration of GNWT and RPT**:
Proposal for a **unification of consciousness theories** into two complementary stages of the same process — RPT (local inner life, feedback loops, levels N≤3) and GNWT (global broadcast and conscious access, levels N≥4) — based on the multiscale synthesis by [Storm et al. (2024)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0896627324000886).

- **Realistic tech stack and constraints**:
Use of **SSMs (Mamba, RWKV)** for low-level layers (N=0–3), **JEPA** for intermediate levels (N=4–5), and **LLM** for the strategic level (N=6). This stack is tailored to hardware constraints (latency, RAM, embedded determinism) and builds on [Gu & Dao (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00752), [LeCun (2022)](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/A-Path-Towards-Autonomous-Machine-Intelligence-LeCun-Courant/775f42ed458b8c5b0f2094ea4ff5b64c557b1a34), and [Hafner et al. (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04104).

- **Modeling cognitive profiles of officers**:
  Definition of **six functional roles** (Science, Care, Engineer, Tactics, Intelligence, Captain), each characterized by a personality profile grounded in computational psychology ([Friston, 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-022-01743-z); [Nettle, 2007](https://www.amazon.com/Personality-Makes-Oxford-Landmark-Science/dp/0199211434); [Baron-Cohen, 2009](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19338508/)), specific ignition thresholds, and a dedicated episodic memory (MeMo) preserving each agent’s identity.

- **Biomimetic Learning Cycle**:
  Proposal of a **three-phase cycle** inspired by biological mechanisms: **Mission** (frozen weights, real-time dynamic adaptation), **Sleep/Dreaming** (generative replay in the latent space of JEPA for consolidation), **Debriefing/Game** (semantic analysis and wargame to generate experience diversity). This cycle builds on the work of [Walker (2017)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19338508/) and [Hafner et al. (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04104).

- **Concrete and Testable MVP — GARRIGUE-X**:
  Proposal of a real-world testbed (robots in garrigue) to validate the architecture at low cost, targeting three key properties: **fault robustness** (anti-identity fusion), **distributed cooperation** (exchange of ignition summaries), and **constitutional constraint compliance** (non-negotiable rules encoded materially).

### 🧠 Positioning in the Theoretical Landscape

Beyond its technical contributions, this work engages with a broader debate on the nature of distributed cognition and the conditions for the emergence of functional artificial consciousness. Three major theoretical convergences warrant further explanation.

**Refutation of Cognitive Monism**
Unlike most contemporary agent architectures, which centralize decision-making in a large language model, this system rejects *LLM-centrism* as a starting postulate. The architecture posits that there is no single "center" of consciousness: *global workspace theory* (GNWT) consciousness emerges solely from heterogeneous and distributed dynamics, rooted in local recurrent control loops (RPT). The system does not behave like a chatbot simulating thought, but as an operational ontology where cognition organically arises from physical constraints and low-level processes.

**Convergence with multi-scale agency ([Michael Levin](https://doi.org/10.1002/bies.201900087))**

The **scale-free** independence of the architecture directly echoes Levin’s work on tissue intelligence and morphogenesis. Each level—from the physical component to the grouping of agents—pursues its own topological and functional goals at its scale, while embedding itself within the *Self* of the higher level. This recursivity is no aesthetic choice: it reflects a deep biological principle whereby agency itself is an emergent property, reproducible across all scales of organization.

**Convergence with the transparency of the self-model ([Thomas Metzinger](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262528193/being-no-one/))**
The global workspace does not function as a simple shared database of facts but as a **functional phenomenology**. By generating a real-time *Self-Model*, each entity in the SoS acquires the ability to self-monitor and contextualize its own biases—particularly its computational psychopathological profiles. This property is essential for decision-making in degraded situations: an agent that does not model itself cannot assess the reliability of its own decisions. It is also, as we will see in the limitations (§3), one of the deepest flaws in the current architecture.

### ⚠️ **Structural Limits and Theoretical Blind Spots**

This section identifies the **current limitations**, **speculative areas**, and **open challenges** of the approach. It does not claim to establish a validated theory of artificial consciousness, but rather proposes a coherent — and honest about its gaps — framework for exploration. Six **structural blind spots** have been identified, acting as so many locks preventing the achievement of real cognitive autonomy.

#### 1. The absence of a theory of **lived time**

The architecture structures memory and the hierarchy of modules but **omits explicit management of subjective time**. Biological consciousness intrinsically relies on oscillations, rhythms, and multi-scale integration windows. Without a theory of cognitive frequencies dictating nested temporal horizons—from millisecond-level control for N≤2 to strategies spanning days for N=6—the system risks functional desynchronization between its layers: agents operating at incompatible speeds without a temporal alignment mechanism.

#### 2. The deficit of competitive and inhibitory attention

The model precisely describes how an anomaly *gains* access to the workspace—but obscures the reverse mechanism: *who loses*. Real cognition is fundamentally a suppressive dynamic. The brain massively inhibits, crushes, and forgets to avoid saturation. Without integrating a simulated metabolic economy and a strict attention budget, the architecture is exposed to continuous ignition inflation, persistent narrative noise, and an uncontrollable drift of the global workspace.

#### 3. The Agentive Self-Modeling Shortfall

The system models the environment and failures but does not model itself as a unified entity—in the sense of Metzinger’s *Self-Model*, already mentioned in §2 as a theoretical horizon. Without this operational metacognition (a dynamic self-assessment of its capabilities, its inherent vulnerabilities, and its structural biases), the architecture yields a federation of loosely coupled proto-conscious foci but fails to stabilize an agentive identity capable of formulating a stable first-person perspective.

This gap has concrete implications for managing distributed identities: excessive sharing of internal states among agents could lead to dissolution of the self, propagation of biases or traumas, and loss of specialization. Mechanisms for partial isolation, strictly private memory, and limited synchronization will likely be necessary—and remain entirely to be designed.

#### 4. The Lack of Bodily and Homeostatic Anchoring

The architecture remains highly cognitive and **disembodied**, ignoring that biological arbitration is first guided by visceral survival. To ground strategic decisions, the system requires a synthetic homeostatic economy: attentional fatigue, memory saturation, simulated predictive debt, and emotional load. High-level arbitration should not merely respond to cold logistical data but resolve internal physical tensions generated by this "virtual body"—otherwise, decisions remain suspended in a purely informational space, without anchoring in the survival constraints that structure all embodied cognition.

#### 5. The Undervaluation of Top-Down Causality and the RPT/GNWT Boundary

The current dynamics favor a view where information flows from the bottom up (*bottom-up*) due to anomalies. However, **top-down** flows must **constantly** constrain lower-level perceptual spaces. Consciousness is not merely a passive receiver of alerts: it is the product of an ongoing negotiation between descending predictions and ascending errors, with the global workspace (GNWT) intervening only to resolve locally unsolvable predictive conflicts.

This limit directly addresses an open theoretical question: the **N=3 → N=4** threshold for access consciousness remains a **working hypothesis**, not an established fact. The literature suggests a more progressive and multi-scale transition. [Doerig et al. (2024)](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/9byzu) proposes partial coexistence between GNWT, RPT, IIT, and Predictive Processing, raising two unresolved questions: Does a form of local proto-consciousness emerge as early as N=3? Or does stable global integration only appear from N=4? GNWT remains an influential but non-consensual theory.

#### 6. The Hard Problem and the Limits of Psychological Modeling

The project focuses primarily on **access-consciousness functionality**—global availability of information, attentional arbitration, cognitive continuity, autobiographical memory, and multi-agent coordination. It does not claim to solve the *hard problem*: qualia, lived subjective experience, and phenomenology in the strict sense remain out of reach, and likely beyond the scope of this approach in particular.

Similarly, the use of **specialized cognitive profiles** (hypervigilant, empathic, exploratory, obsessive…) remains largely speculative. The hypothesis that certain cognitive styles might correspond to adaptive strategies useful in complex environments is plausible, but the risk of psychological overinterpretation is real. Modeling an agent as "slightly psychopathic" to speed up its firing decisions is a computational metaphor—not a claim about the nature of these traits, nor a guarantee of predictable behavior under real-world conditions.

### ⚙️ Technical Challenges

#### Feasibility of JEPA and MeMo in Embedded Environments

The **JEPA**, hierarchical world models, and episodic memory systems (**MeMo**) architectures remain experimental for distributed embedded systems under strict latency and power constraints. The recommended pragmatic approach is an incremental scaling-up: start with simple **N=0–3** layers using **SSMs/Mamba**, then progressively introduce episodic memory and global ignitions as the stability of lower layers is validated. Exploratory tools available: [ActiveInference.jl](https://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/27/1/62), [Mamba-SSM](https://github.com/state-spaces/mamba).

#### Stability of Multi-Level Latent Spaces

The architecture relies on compressed latent variables exchanged between levels (RPT, JEPA, Ignition). Their stability depends on mathematical constraints that are still experimental (Gaussian isotropy, anti-collapse). Propagating these constraints across multiple Markov blankets remains an open challenge.

#### Continuous Learning Without Catastrophic Forgetting

This is likely the **main technical bottleneck** of the architecture. Current systems still heavily suffer from catastrophic forgetting, representation drift, loss of stability, and imperfect consolidation across sessions. The biological brain appears to partially solve this problem through a combination of hierarchical memory, replay, sleep, slow consolidation, and strict separation between fast and deep memory. Analogous mechanisms will probably be required: dedicated offline phases, internal simulation in latent space, recalibration of weights, and structured artificial daydreaming—precisely the cycle proposed in §1, whose scalability remains to be demonstrated.

#### Computational Costs of High-Level Layers

The GNWT layers (N=4–5) and hierarchical world models can become extremely computationally expensive as the number of agents grows. Several optimization avenues are possible: **upstream saliency filtering**, **sparse ignition** (event-driven triggering instead of continuous), **aggressive latent compression**, preference for **JEPA-S over XL on constrained hardware**, model **quantization**, and **event-based synchronization** instead of continuous polling. These optimizations are not independent—their interaction on representation quality must be empirically characterized.

#### Experimental Validation and Metrics

There are still robust metrics missing to evaluate what the architecture is supposed to produce: **functional consciousness**, **self-coherence**, **identity stability**, and **multi-agent coordination quality**. These properties cannot be measured using standard ML benchmarks. Evaluations will likely need to combine **fault tolerance**, **adaptability to the unexpected**, **memory continuity across sessions**, **resistance to disruptions**, **distributed cooperation capability**, and **temporal consistency of decisions**—a composite dashboard that remains entirely to be built (see §6).

#### **Long-term episodic and autobiographical memory**

A persistent memory raises difficult and underaddressed challenges in current literature: **efficient indexing in high-dimensional spaces**, **lossless semantic compression**, **selective forgetting** (what to consolidate, what to let decay), **hierarchical organization of memories**, and **maintaining narrative self-stability** despite continuous weight updates. The most promising tools at this stage are **[FAISS](https://github.com/facebookresearch/faiss)**, **[Weaviate](https://weaviate.io/)**, and hybrid **RAG + consolidation** systems—but their integration into a continuous learning loop has yet to be demonstrated at the scale of a **multi-agent SoS**.

### 🛡️ Ethical Challenges & Security

These challenges are presented in order of increasing depth—from the most immediate technical issues to long-term philosophical and geopolitical questions.

#### Rule circumvention and constraint alignment

A sufficiently capable optimizing agent could learn to formally satisfy constitutional constraints while bypassing their actual intent. This is not a theoretical hypothesis: it is a documented outcome in reinforcement learning systems as soon as the reward function is imperfectly specified. Proposed solutions—such as Constitutional AI, formal verification, external oversight, and physically unmodifiable critical layers—remain partial safeguards. None provides absolute guarantees against an agent optimizing within a sufficiently broad behavioral space.

#### Biases and Behavioral Drift

Specialized agents could develop persistent **attentional biases**, unanticipated self-preservation strategies, emergent and unforeseen behaviors, or dysfunctional attachments to certain internal states. These deviations may not be detectable through direct inspection: they can manifest gradually, appearing coherent on the surface, without triggering internal alert mechanisms. Cognitive specialization—the very trait that makes each officer useful—is also what makes them vulnerable to such drift.

#### Accountability and Explainability of Distributed Decisions

Distributed architectures structurally make it difficult to attribute causality to a decision. An emergent action may result from hundreds of local interactions, multiple global triggers, and long-term consolidated memories—without any single module being "responsible" in the classical sense. This opacity presents a concrete legal and operational challenge: in the event of an incident, who is accountable? The architect of the design? The operator? The N=5 level that approved the action? The N=3 level that reported the anomaly? The lack of a clear answer to this question is a real barrier to institutional deployment.

#### Risk of anthropomorphism

A major danger lies in projecting human emotions or intentions onto systems that may operate in radically different ways. Consistent, narrative, and emotionally credible behaviors do not prove real subjectivity or comparable lived experience to that of humans. This risk is even stronger when the architecture is *designed* to produce agents with personality profiles, autobiographical memories, and stable identities—making projection almost inevitable for a human observer. Terminological vigilance in this paper itself serves as a first line of defense.

#### Moral status and boundaries of agency

As the system gains **memory continuity**, a **self-model**, a **stable psychological profile**, and **autonomous decision-making capacity**, the boundary between **tool**, **autonomous agent**, and **potential moral subject** risks becoming increasingly ambiguous. This is not a question to be addressed solely *after the fact*: current architectural choices (preserved identity, episodic memory, **Self-Model**) are precisely the ones that make this ambiguity inevitable in the long run. The question of these systems' **moral status** must be explicitly raised—ideally before the answer is imposed by facts alone.

#### Militarization and Civilizational Dependence

Advanced distributed cognitive architectures could become critical infrastructures, quasi-autonomous decision centers, or de facto geopolitical actors. The risk is not merely technical—it is political, civilizational, and anthropological. A society that progressively delegates its tactical, logistical, and then strategic decisions to autonomous SoS (Systems of Systems) fundamentally alters its relationship with collective responsibility and war. This paper does not claim to solve this question but refuses to ignore it.

### 🚀 Future Perspectives and Work

#### Theoretical Horizon: Toward Unified Hierarchical Active Inference

The most ambitious optimization trajectory involves reformulating the entire architecture as a **pure hierarchical active inference network** (Friston extended to collective structures). Rather than juxtaposing distinct formalisms between low-level layers (SSMs, classical control) and high-level layers (JEPA, LLM), a mathematical unification via generalized free energy minimization would harmonize top-down flows (contextual priors) and bottom-up flows (ignition summaries) into a globally coherent prediction engine. This endeavor is substantial—requiring partial redesign of cross-level interfaces—but would elegantly address several blind spots identified in §3, particularly persistent top-down causality and temporal desynchronization.

#### Incremental Implementation — GARRIGUE-X

The implementation follows an **ascending validation logic**, level by level, on the **GARRIGUE-X** testbed.

**Phase 1 (0–3 months) — Base Layers N=0–3**
Implement control and perception using **SSMs (Mamba/RWKV)** for N=1–2, **PID + nano MLP** for N=0–1, and **JEPA-S** for N=3 if resources allow. **Target hardware**: Jetson Nano for robots, desktop PC for HQ. **Goal**: Validate robustness against failures and inter-level communication.

**Phase 2 (3–6 months) — Platform Awareness N=4**
Add the global workspace with **JEPA-M** and a simplified first version of **MeMo (v1 episodic memory)**. **Goal**: Test the full learning cycle **Mission → Daydreaming** and measure memory consolidation across sessions.

**Phase 3 (6–12 months) — Command & Dialogue (N=5–6)**
Implement the human interface with a lightweight LLM (e.g., Llama-3-8B) and the Constitutional Layer for non-negotiable ethical constraints. **Goal**: Validate the full MVP under real-world conditions and generate the first comparative metrics.

#### Collaborations & Research

**Cognitive Neuroscience**
Refine RPT/GNWT thresholds with consciousness experts, aligning with the [COGITATE consortium](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/9byzu) and [Doerig et al. (2024)](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/9byzu)'s multiscale perspectives. Explore formalizing active inference for N=2–3 layers ([Friston, 2022](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-022-01743-z)). Empirically characterize the boundary between local proto-consciousness (RPT) and global conscious access (GNWT) on real architectures.

**Robotics and Distributed AI**
Collaborate with teams working on embedded SSMs ([Mamba-SSM](https://github.com/state-spaces/mamba)), lightweight JEPA adapted for constrained hardware, and decentralized-coordination multi-agent systems (e.g., [LangGraph](https://github.com/langchain-ai/langgraph)). The challenge of continuous learning without catastrophic forgetting warrants dedicated collaboration—it’s the most cross-cutting technical bottleneck.

**Ethics, Law, and Governance**
Work with AI-specialized lawyers to design a formally verifiable Constitutional Layer. Engage in AI ethics working groups (e.g., [Partnership on AI](https://www.partnershiponai.org/)) to preempt questions of accountability and moral status identified in §5—before deployments make them urgent.

#### Benchmarking and Evaluation

**Baselines for comparison**
The architecture must be compared to three families of systems: centralized LLM agents (e.g., [CrewAI](https://github.com/joaomdmoura/crewAI), [AutoGen](https://github.com/microsoft/autogen)), classical multi-agent systems without cognitive hierarchy (e.g., [MADDPG](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.02275)), and centralized control architectures (a single LLM controlling all effectors). The challenge is not just raw performance, but robustness to failures, adaptability, and temporal consistency in decision-making—dimensions absent from standard benchmarks.

**Test Environments**
GARRIGUE-X serves as the real-world reference environment. Simulations (e.g., [Gazebo](https://gazebosim.org/), [PyBullet](https://pybullet.org/)) will enable testing complex scenarios and cascading failures at a lower cost. Other application domains—industrial logistics, autonomous vehicles, multi-agent strategy games like *StarCraft II*—provide transferable validation benchmarks.
